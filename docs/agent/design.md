# Design

- Make the product feel intentional, memorable, and a little surprising.
- Favor strong visual concepts over safe, generic UI patterns.
- Keep designs usable first, playful second, and never at the cost of clarity.

## Principles

- Build around a clear visual direction, not default app aesthetics.
- Use typography, color, spacing, shape, and motion with purpose.
- Prefer compositions that create identity instead of interchangeable screens.
- Keep accessibility AAA-level and maintain strong contrast and focus states.

## Creative Direction

- Encourage playful layouts, distinctive components, and subtle delight.
- Use motion sparingly but meaningfully.
- Break symmetry when it improves hierarchy or personality.
- Avoid bland, overused patterns unless they are the right fit for the task.

## Empty States

- Treat empty states as intentional product moments, not placeholders.
- Show why the state exists and what to do next.
- Use a distinct visual treatment when the empty state is part of the brand story.
- Keep the primary CTA obvious and the copy encouraging.

## Guardrails

- Preserve established design language when extending an existing product area.
- Do not let novelty reduce usability, performance, or readability.
- Ensure every unusual choice has a clear function.

## Review Questions

- Does this feel distinct?
- Does this still work instantly for first-time users?
- Does the visual treatment support the product meaning?
- Is the design accessible in real-world use?
